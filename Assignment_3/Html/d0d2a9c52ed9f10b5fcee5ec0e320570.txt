		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://mamamboga.wordpress.com/2014/05/20/with-twitch-acquisition-youtube-might-finally-make-live-streaming-matter/" rel="bookmark">With Twitch acquisition, YouTube might finally make live streaming&nbsp;matter</a></h1>
	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

		<div class="entry-content">
		<div class="wpcom-reblog-snapshot"> <div class="reblog-post"><p class="reblog-from"><img alt='' src='http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/08bc62ecf138202f06b74dfa01376e74?s=48&#038;d=identicon&#038;r=PG' class='avatar avatar-48' height='48' width='48' />Originally posted on <a href="http://gigaom.com/2014/05/19/with-twitch-acquisition-youtube-might-finally-make-live-streaming-matter">Gigaom</a>:</p><div class="reblogged-content">
<p>YouTube (S GOOG) hasn’t confirmed <a href="http://gigaom.com/2014/05/18/youtube-is-reportedly-buying-video-game-live-streaming-site-twitch-for-1-billion/">this weekend’s reports that it is about to acquire video game live streaming site Twitch</a> for $1 billion in cash, but I’d imagine that the news made folks working at YouTube, as well as many of the service’s publishers, very happy. That’s because a Twitch acquisition could help YouTube finally get a foothold in the live video space.</p>

<p>Live video has been a complicated subject for YouTube. The Google-owned video service <a href="http://gigaom.com/2010/09/13/watch-out-ustream-justin-tv-youtube-starts-live-streaming/">started to dabble with live streaming all the way back in 2010</a>, and gradually opened up the ability to go live to almost all of its publishers in the following years. But aside from a few marquee events, the quality of live content on the platform has been questionable. Discovery has been a huge headache, and efforts to professionalize live streaming have been cut short. At this point, YouTube Live is barely alive.</p>
