<p class="home-title">eleven</p>
<p class="home-author">by tom rogers</p>
<p class="home-desc">the journey of a boy turning eleven on 9/11</p>
<h6 class="home-quote">“A thoroughly engaging piece about a very difficult subject. Very well written – funny and emotional from beginning to end. And I loved Radar.”</h6>
<p class="home-quote-credit" style="text-align: right;">— Alan Horn, <em>Chairman</em>, The Walt Disney Studios</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a title="travel down page" href="#t1"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3344" src="http://www.eleventhebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/arrow-down.png" alt="travel down page" width="70" height="70" /></a></p>
<div id="t1"></div>
<div class="background-block" style="background-color:#ffffff; background-image:url(http://www.eleventhebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/white.jpg); margin-top:70px; margin-bottom:0px; padding-top:40px; padding-bottom:0px; text-align:left;"><div class="background-block-container">
<h2 style="text-align: center;">The Book</h2>
<div class="column column-1-4">
<a title="Buy The Book" href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/099118100X" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-3307" src="http://www.eleventhebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/book-cover.png" alt="book-cover" /></a><br />
</div>
<div class="column column-3-4 column-last">
<div class="spacer" style="height:10px"></div>
Alex Douglas always wanted to be a hero. But nothing heroic ever happened to Alex. Nothing, that is, until his eleventh birthday. When Alex rescues a stray dog as a birthday gift to himself, he doesn&#8217;t think his life can get much better. Radar, his new dog, pretty much feels the same way. But this day has bigger things in store for both of them.</p>
<p>This is a story about bullies and heroes. About tragedy and hope. About enemies with two legs and friends with four, and pesky little sisters and cranky old men, and an unexpected lesson in kindness delivered with a slice of pizza.</p>
<p>This is <em>Eleven</em>: the journey of a boy turning eleven on 9/11.<br />
</div><div class="clear"></div>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">The Author</h2>
<div class="column column-1-4"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3360" src="http://www.eleventhebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Tom-Rogers-Headshot.jpg" alt="Tom Rogers Headshot" /></div>
<div class="column column-3-4 column-last">
<div class="spacer" style="height:10px"></div>
Tom Rogers is a novelist and the screenwriter of numerous animated films, including <em>The Lion King 1½</em>, <em>Kronk&#8217;s New Groove</em>, <em>LEGO: The Adventures of Clutch Powers</em>, and Disney&#8217;s <em>Secret of the Wings</em> and the upcoming <em>Legend of the NeverBeast</em>. Originally from Texas, he now lives in Los Angeles with his wife and tango partner, Jennifer. <em>Eleven</em> is his first novel for young adults.<br />
</div><div class="clear"></div>

<h2 style="text-align: center;">Chapter Excerpts</h2>
<div class="column column-1-3">
<p style="text-align: left;"><i style="color:#b9b9b9; font-size:99px" class="moon-steps"></i>
<h6>You Smell Like a Monkey</h6>
<p><em>Chapter 4 excerpt</em></p>
<p align="left">8:05 a.m.</p>
<p>The yellow bus rumbled through a tumbledown section of town, an industrial no-man’s-land of railroad tracks, warehouses, and empty factories with broken windows and rusted steel roofs. The bus didn’t stop here, because no kids lived here.</p>
<p>The sound of the tires changed to a high-pitched hum as the bus crossed a tall bridge that soared over a rail yard and a narrow river. Alex turned to look out the window &#8230;</p>

<h6>A Dog&#8217;s Life</h6>
<p><em>Chapter 10 excerpt</em></p>
<p>10:34 a.m.</p>
<p>“Here, Max.”</p>
<p>Alex called to the dog, who had stopped to sniff a light pole. The dog didn’t respond but moved on to investigate a trash can.</p>
<p>Nunu looked at him funny. “Who’s Max?”</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">“I’m trying out my top ten dog names. Does he look like a Max to you?”</p>
<p>Alex made a face. “Muffin?” &#8230;</p>

<h6>The Man in the White Shirt</h6>
<p><em>Chapter 13 excerpt</em></p>
<p>10:02 a.m.</p>
<p>The Man in the White Shirt staggered through the choking gray cloud. He couldn’t see where he was going. He couldn’t see anything.</p>
<p>He nearly crashed into a light pole that appeared a foot in front of him.</p>
<p>He heard others around him, panting as they ran.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Someone slammed into him from behind and then disappeared into the cloud &#8230;</p>
