<h1 class="title">Greetings From Twitterland</h1>	<div class="entry">
	    <p><img title="Reach your audience the new-fashioned way." alt="Reach your audience the new-fashioned way." src="http://www.raindance.org/site/content/gt/2e6431e2d612aa974378abd1a7fcac6a.gif" width="462" height="25" border="0" /><a href="http://www.raindance.org/site/index.php?aid=933"><br />
</a></p>
<hr size="2" width="100%" />
<p><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-17193" alt="Raindance independent film festival" src="http://www.raindance.org/wp-content/uploads/Raindance.png" width="200" height="200" />This is the ultra secret Twitter page that tells me you have arrived from the Raindance Twitter account which I started in May 2009. When I started it, I was dreading the experience. I didn&#8217;t want another item on my daily to-do list. But I&#8217;ve become a convert and manage the Twitter account myself, along with occassional help from the interns.</p>
<p>I founded Raindance way back in 1992. John Major had just won an election for the Tories and Princess Diana was still alive.</p>
<p>The goal of Raindance was to provide information and advice for film and television makers here in Britain. I organised a series of workshops and seminars and flew over some of the big names from Hollywood. Dov Simens is still coming to London after all these years.</p>
<p>Soon however, filmmakers started making movies, and back in the early &#8217;90&#8242;s, there wasn&#8217;t a British festival to show them &#8211; so I started the Raindance Film Festival. In 1995 we had one of the <a href="http://state51.co.uk/raindance/events.html">first websites</a> of any company in the UK &#8211; it was 4 pages long!</p>
<p>In 1998 I started the <a href="http://www.bifa.org.uk">British Independent Film Awards</a> which has grown to become one of the main event in the British film industry year.<br />
In 2004 I co-founded <a title="Raindance Toronto" href="http://www.raindance.org/toronto/">Raindance Canada</a><br />
In 2007 I co-founded <a href="http://www.raindance.tv">Raindance.tv</a> &#8211; an IPTV web channel.<br />
In 2010 we opened offices being in <a href="http://www.raindance.org/site/Raindance-New-York-City-home-of-independent-film">New York</a>, <a href="http://www.raindance.org/berlin/">Berlin</a>, <a title="Raindance Brussels" href="http://www.raindance.org/brussels/">Brussels</a> and Budapest.<br />
In 2011 we launched the <a title="Raindance Postgraduate Degree" href="http://www.raindance.org/postgraduate-film-degree/">Raindance Postgraduate Film Degree</a> with Staffordshire University and the <a href="http://www.independentfilmtrust.org/">Independent Film Trust</a>.<br />
In 2012 we moved into the Raindance Film Centre just off Trafalgar Square, developed on Online Postgraduate Film Degree and celebrated the 20th year of the <a href="http://www.raindance.co.uk/site/festival">Raindance Film Festival</a>.</p>
<p>I couldn&#8217;t ever have done any of this without the help and support of a great many superbly talented people who work for next-to-nothing on the Raindance team.</p>
<p>Finally, it&#8217;s a big thanks to you for reading this. It&#8217;s you telling your friends about Raindance, RT&#8217;ing our Tweets, favouriting our posts and sending in your comments that makes me get up in the morning!</p>
<p><img alt="" src="http://www.raindance.org/site/picture/upload/image/LOGOS/elliot_grove_signature.gif" width="225" height="107" align="left" border="0" hspace="5" vspace="3" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Elliot Grove</p>
<h3>The Theory Behind Twitter</h3>
<p>Imagine you are walking down the hallway of your student residence hall. You pass a neighbour and say, &#8220;what&#8217;s up?&#8221; and he says, &#8220;nothing.&#8221; Then you keep walking. Later you&#8217;re walking through the kitchen at work, someone is filling their coffee. &#8220;How&#8217;re things?&#8221; she asks. &#8220;Busy, but good,&#8221; you respond and keep on walking to the bathroom.</p>
<p>If either person hadn&#8217;t greeted you, you go back to your dorm room or office and wonder, &#8220;what happened? why the cold shoulder?&#8221; Those small bits of conversation are called phatic communication. Alone those bits are meaningless, but together they build a relationship. Phatic communication keeps the doors open for more communication.</p>
<p><a href="mailto:elliot@raindance.co.uk?subject=From%20the%20Twitter%20Landing%20PAge&amp;body=This%20is%20my%20private%20email%20account.%20If%20I%20don%27t%20answer%20right%20away%20its%20because%20I%20am%20traveling%2C%20or%20because%20I%20am%20disorganised.%20If%20I%20haven%27t%20responded%20in%2048%20hours%2C%20drag%20this%20out%20of%20your%20sent%20mail%20folder%20and%20click%20%27Resend%27.">Get in Touch</a> with me</p>
<p>You can connect with me personally on <a href="http://www.facebook.com/home.php?">Facebook</a> and <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/profile?viewProfile=&amp;key=4932607&amp;locale=en_US&amp;trk=tab_pro">LinkedIn</a>!<br />
Raindance Film Festival has a <a href="http://www.facebook.com/RaindanceFilmFestLondon">Facebook</a> page. We co-founded a registered charity: <strong>The Independent Film Trust</strong>, which raises money to promote and teach filmmaking to the disadvantaged. Please <a href="http://www.independentfilmtrust.org/">give generously</a>.</p>
