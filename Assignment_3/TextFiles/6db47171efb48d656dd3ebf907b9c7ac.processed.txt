   [396]Question: 79 ford f250 460 wants to start but dies?

   Posted: 29 Sep 2014 04:36 PM PDT

   Your starting resistor is open. That means there is a big resistor that
   goes to the coil, and during starting, the resistor is bypassed. When
   the car is turned to "run" the resistor delivers less power to the coil
   because that's the way it works. The resistor essentially allows a huge
   spark to be delivered during cranking, then goes to normal as the
   engine runs. If the resistor is open, this is exactly what happens.
