   [57]tumblr_n7n316dRz41tp98tmo1_500 As I get older, I find the weekends
   to be more magical. We go through the whole week full of
   tension—mental, physical, emotional—and from Friday night to Sunday
   night, we get to release. We can abandon expectations. We can let go of
   failures and stress. We can forget schedules. Instead of tiring
   ourselves out with overly-planned weekends, what if we all took time to
   truly relax?

   This weekend, let’s give it a try. Lately, I’ve found myself longing
   for meditative practices, like yoga and hot baths or nighttime swims.
   Think about what you want, and do it. I don’t mean hard-work goals. I
   mean what brings joy to your soul? Seek those things out and hold them
   close on the weekend. It could be as simple as making a cup of tea or
   as bold as hiking a new path.

   If we can embrace those things that make us smile from the inside out
   for three nights, maybe we can even learn to bring souljoy into our
   everyday. Here are some things that have me feeling inspired, taken
   from Sarah’s gorgeous blog.

[76]Gemini Season Forever

   Posted on [77]September 2, 2014 by [78]Isabella

   [79]1

   [80]IMG_4691 [81]IMG_4703 [82]IMG_4695 [83]IMG_4694

   Hey, it’s September!

   A rapid fire Google search tells me that means it is Virgo season.
   Whatever that means for you, woohoo. I am not taken by astrology by any
   means, but I have found the word gemini to describe me well, despite
   its crunchy granola insinuations. Gemini is the Latin word for “twins,”
   which wraps up, very simply, my nature. By this I don’t mean I have
   bipolar disorder because I have never been to a psychiatrist, but that
   the two sides of my personality are so distinct. The academic and
   creative sides often work isolated from one another, but I am very
   thankful when they decide to collaborate. The intro- and extroverted
   parts of me, as well.

   That being said, I went all heart eyes emoji when I found the TAudrey
   “Gemini” horoscope nameplate. I very well could have emblazoned my
   collarbones with my birth name or social media handle, but the creepers
   and stalkers of the world should not have that privilege. I would have
   been fine with the mailperson dropping the dainty chain in the mailbox
   like it’s hot. I would have been dismayed, but they have no reason to
   value the handmade beauty of it. However, the lovely Tiffany over at
   TAudrey sent it in the same way your favorite aunt would send you a
   mood ring for your birthday in third grade—enclosed in a charming pink
   box, placed in a sheer drawstring pouch and accompanied by a
   handwritten note.

   I wore mine to the beach this weekend, layered with a longer drop
   pendant chain to emulate Rihanna and fail at emulating Rihanna. Despite
   this, there is nothing quite like a very personal piece of jewelry, and
   TAudrey has your back.
