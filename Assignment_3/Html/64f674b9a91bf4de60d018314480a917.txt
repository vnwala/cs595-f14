		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/27/cuban-magic-at-latin-jazz-festival-2014/" rel="bookmark">Cuban magic at Latin Jazz Festival&nbsp;2014</a></h1>
				<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="posted-on">Posted on <a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/27/cuban-magic-at-latin-jazz-festival-2014/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-09-27T11:21:00+00:00">September 27, 2014</time><time class="updated" datetime="2014-09-27T14:46:24+00:00">September 27, 2014</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://london-nbeyond.com/author/aggatha79/">aggatha79</a></span></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
	<a class="post-thumbnail" href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/27/cuban-magic-at-latin-jazz-festival-2014/">
		<img width="772" height="579" src="http://londonnbeyond.files.wordpress.com/2014/09/unnamed.jpg?w=772" class="attachment-edin-featured-image wp-post-image" alt="Eliane Correa @ Pizza Express Jazz Club Soho" />	</a>


	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>For a lover of latin music, London&#8217;s Latin Jazz Festival is always great news. On its second year, at <a href="http://www.pizzaexpresslive.com/jazzList.aspx">Pizza Express Soho Jazz Club,</a> the festival runs from 23 Sept &#8211; 27 Sept. I went to see <span style="color:#ff0000;"><strong>Eliane Correa &amp; En El Project.</strong></span></p>
<p>The show opened with a piano-percussion duo featuring curator <span style="color:#ff0000;"><strong>Alex Wilson</strong></span> on the piano and Venezuelan percussion Master <span style="color:#ff0000;"><strong>Edwin Sanz</strong></span> which built up an amazing atmosphere leading to the sector act. I was already enchanted!</p>
<p>Half Argentinian, half Cuban &#8211; Eliane is a very talented musician and musical director. The En El Project is a result of Eliane&#8217;s collaboration with a team of brilliant musicians working in a studio in Havana for the last few years. This was the very first live performance.</p>
<p>It was 2 hours of beautiful cuban jazz, mixed with soul, sounds as well as Eliane&#8217;s interpretation of cuban classics with a jazzy twist. I could not get enough. The album is expected in late October and if you are into jazz you should definitely check them out.</p>
<p>I hope London&#8217;s Latin Jazz Festival grows stronger and longer!</p>
<p><em>And another thing&#8230;</em></p>
<p>I never understood the concept of having a full blown meal while artists are giving their best to perform (and this comes from a Greek where music and eating is usually an interwoven experience) unless it is a street fiesta or something else of a similar objective. At Pizza Express Jazz Club I was trying for the first few songs to focus on the music while having to hear people&#8217;s cutlery mastering pizza eating. Or that time at <a href="http://www.pizzaexpresslive.com/jazzList.aspx">Boisdale</a>  that I had to sit through the loudness of a city bankers group dinner while the focus was the beautiful beats of Brazilian artist Monica Vasconcelos.</p>
<p>Music and especially jazz is to be enjoyed with a fine glass of wine or scotch not lamb chops. Venues should really consider putting a time cap on the dinner timings so that everybody has a good experience. The diners, us, and the musicians.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/21/the-raouls-institution/" rel="bookmark">The &#8216;Raoul&#8217;s&#8217; institution</a></h1>
				<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="posted-on">Posted on <a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/21/the-raouls-institution/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-09-21T15:03:54+00:00">September 21, 2014</time><time class="updated" datetime="2014-09-27T11:35:09+00:00">September 27, 2014</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://london-nbeyond.com/author/aggatha79/">aggatha79</a></span></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	
	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>It&#8217;s a comfort that there are some places in London that offer the same ambience, quality of food and service throughout the years without messing with &#8216;forced style&#8217; deco or menu changes. Sometimes you just don&#8217;t like change. If something really works, why spoil it? <a href="http://www.raoulsgourmet.com/notting-hill.html">Raoul&#8217;s</a> in Notting Hill is one of them. This is my go to place for a hearty brunch, great service and a relaxed neighbourhood atmosphere. Located in the heart of Notting Hill, this brasserie style restaurant offers dishes based on ingredients of high quality. The fresh fruit smoothies is always a winner and breakfast dishes is what most Notting Hillers are coming here for in the weekends. The place is quite hidden, it is popular amongst the locals (as you really need to know where to head to) and this provides quite a serene atmosphere. It never fails to get me in a great mood and start of the day. 9 years after my first visit, it is still going strong. Have a look at the<a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/best-for-brunch/"> full list </a>of my recommendations for great brunch places in London.</p>
			</div><!-- .entry-content -->

		<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/20/finding-old-shoreditch-at-dalstons-street-feast/" rel="bookmark">Finding old Shoreditch at Dalston&#8217;s Street&nbsp;Feast</a></h1>
				<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="posted-on">Posted on <a href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/20/finding-old-shoreditch-at-dalstons-street-feast/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-09-20T23:21:14+00:00">September 20, 2014</time><time class="updated" datetime="2014-09-27T11:36:26+00:00">September 27, 2014</time></a></span><span class="byline"> by <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://london-nbeyond.com/author/aggatha79/">aggatha79</a></span></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

	<a class="post-thumbnail" href="http://london-nbeyond.com/2014/09/20/finding-old-shoreditch-at-dalstons-street-feast/">
		<img width="772" height="579" src="http://londonnbeyond.files.wordpress.com/2014/09/img_5628.jpg?w=772" class="attachment-edin-featured-image wp-post-image" alt="IMG_5628" />	</a>


	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>I resisted visiting Dalston for a while only because I dislike all this hype words of &#8216;the new x&#8217;. I must admit, Dalston is indeed quite close to what Shoreditch used to be circa 2005. In reality nothing will ever be &#8216;that&#8217; Shoreditch, with its unpolished yet charming style, lack of anything called &#8216;hipster&#8217; and forever unpretentiousness. But for all of us who are reminiscing those good old days, Dalston is as good as it can get!</p>
<figure id="attachment_398" style="width: 700px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://londonnbeyond.files.wordpress.com/2014/09/img_5626.jpg"><img class="wp-image-398 size-full" src="http://londonnbeyond.files.wordpress.com/2014/09/img_5626.jpg?w=700&#038;h=525" alt="IMG_5626" width="700" height="525" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Street Feast &#8211; Dalston Yard</figcaption></figure>
<p><a href="http://www.streetfeastlondon.com">Street Feast</a> in Dalston Yard was the reason we made our way East. Street Feast is a nomadic night market bringing together restaurants, street food pop ups, exotic cocktails and a lot of atmosphere. Street food markets is the new craze in London and in the era of the &#8216;foodie trend&#8217; I am quite relieved this craze finally made its way to the Capital. Mostly because you don&#8217;t have to drag yourself in an expensive restaurant, put up with the attitude, to enjoy good quality food and end up being tensed for most of the night. Street food markets give you this opportunity to enjoy the good food in a relaxed setting and actually &#8216;hang out&#8217;. Street food markets is all about the food but it isn&#8217;t at the same time. It is the new social scene. If you get a chance to go make sure you stop by <a href="http://smokestak.co.uk">Smokestak</a> (don&#8217;t think I&#8217;ve ever had as good as a pork bun) as well as <a href="http://www.rolawala.com">Rola Wala</a> for imaginative Indian street food. I already look forward to the new host venue of Street Feast. Will be there!</p>
