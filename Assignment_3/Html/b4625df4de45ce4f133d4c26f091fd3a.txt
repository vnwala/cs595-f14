                                            <h2><a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/98284900082/episode-031-home-teams-with-gregg-gethard">Episode 031: Home Teams (with Gregg Gethard)</a></h2>
                                            
                                            <div class="body-text">
                                                <p><strong><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850">Subscribe</a><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850"><strong> on iTun</strong>es!</a> (and rate it 5 stars!)</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://traffic.libsyn.com/dryheatpodcast/SideStreetsEpisode031.mp3">Download Episode 031: Home Teams (with Gregg Gethard)</a> (via LibSyn)</strong></p>
<p><iframe height="360" scrolling="no" src="//html5-player.libsyn.com/embed/episode/id/3081924/height/360/width/500/theme/standard/direction/no/autoplay/no/autonext/no/thumbnail/yes/preload/no/no_addthis/no/" width="500"></iframe></p>
<p>We&#8217;re talking about sports this week.</p>
<p><img alt="image" src="http://media.tumblr.com/06f68df026050036f7634d0512bd7afa/tumblr_inline_ncbtc9w4m11szsob5.jpg"/></p>
<p>I&#8217;ve been a sports fan since&#8230;well&#8230;since I was able to form memories, I suppose. I was brought up around it and it&#8217;s in my blood. No matter how frustrating or infuriating or absurd sports fandom gets (look no further than <strong>JD &amp; The Straight Shot</strong> for peak absurdity), nobody completely shakes the things they loved as kids. My favorite sport (by a mile) is basketball, and that&#8217;s primarily what we cover in this episode. But any sports conversation inevitably turns into a conversation about how these games impact the atmosphere of the towns in which we live.</p>
<p>Our guest this week is <strong>Gregg Gethard</strong>, a fellow basketball obsessive and sports fan. We talk about our experiences playing and following basketball, as well as a look into how a town&#8217;s mood can fluctuate depending on a team&#8217;s success (as weird and irrational as it is). We also talk about his brief stint as the <strong>Greggulator</strong> on The Best Show on WFMU and how much we&#8217;d give up just to be able to dunk a basketball for an hour.</p>
<p>Gethard is a comedian, writer (<a href="http://theclassical.org/articles/leaving-it-on-the-field-in-coatesville">check out this phenomenal piece</a> on Coatesville&#8217;s football team for The Classical), <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/Lifestyle/man-publishes-series-yelp-reviews/story?id=24883140">Yelp novelist</a>, and all-around good guy. You might know his brother, <a href="http://thechrisgethardshow.com/">Chris</a>, who is one of the funniest comic performers working today. Follow Gregg on twitter (<a href="https://twitter.com/holdingcourtpod">@holdingcourtpod</a>).</p>
<p>Also in this episode,<strong> I stake my claim to become the University of Arizona Men&#8217;s Basketball public address announcer</strong>. Let&#8217;s make it happen!</p>

                                            <h2><a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/97708522942/episode-030-never-known-this-til-now-with-human">Episode 030: Never Known This Til Now (with Human Behavior)</a></h2>
                                            
                                            <div class="body-text">
                                                <p><strong><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850">Subscribe</a><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850"><strong> on iTun</strong>es!</a> (and rate it 5 stars!)</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://traffic.libsyn.com/dryheatpodcast/SideStreetsEpisode030.mp3">Download Episode 030: Never Known This Til Now (with Human Behavior)</a> (via LibSyn)</strong></p>
<p><iframe height="360" scrolling="no" src="//html5-player.libsyn.com/embed/episode/id/3069635/height/360/width/500/theme/standard/direction/no/autoplay/no/autonext/no/thumbnail/yes/preload/no/no_addthis/no/" width="500"></iframe></p>
<p>Music is magic. It can creep under your skin, worm its way into your brain, and make you feel/remember things you can&#8217;t put words to. Tucson&#8217;s Human Behavior specializes in making audiences feel things &#8212; things they don&#8217;t typically feel when they&#8217;re at a bar watching live music.</p>
<p><img alt="image" src="http://media.tumblr.com/c96c779b9fb3ac4efddc6280da6cc313/tumblr_inline_nc06fzZk4m1szsob5.png"/></p>
<p>In this episode, we talk to <strong>Andres Parada</strong> (the lead singer/songwriter) and <strong>Chris Pierce</strong> (bass) to find out what makes this band tick, as well as how Andy is able to address such sensitive topics (mental illness, depression, suicide) on stage. His music is stark, haunting, and one-of-a-kind. If you like Will Oldham/Bonnie Prince Billy, Kurt Vile, Father John Misty, etc., you&#8217;ll love Human Behavior. There is also a strong connection to the music of Daniel Johnston or Roky Erickson. I wish I could tell you more about it, but words don&#8217;t really cover it. You really need to see them live to know what I&#8217;m talking about.</p>
<p>Lucky for you, Human Behavior is hitting the road. They&#8217;re embarking upon an ambitious American tour. If they&#8217;re playing your city, make sure you go see them. <strong>[<a href="http://www.humanbehaviormusic.com/EARS.html">Click here for a full list of tour dates</a>]</strong></p>
<p>Human Behavior has three full-length records (Golgotha, Eat The Wind, The Behavior of Death in Mower County) and a four-track EP (Sonora) that you can find <strong><a href="http://humanbehaviormusic.bandcamp.com/">at their bandcamp page</a></strong>. It&#8217;s all name-your-own-price, so check it out &#8212; you have nothing to lose and everything to gain!</p>

                                            <div class="body-text">
                                                <p><strong><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850">Subscribe</a><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850"><strong> on iTun</strong>es!</a> (and rate it 5 stars!)</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://traffic.libsyn.com/dryheatpodcast/SideStreetsEpisode029.mp3" target="_blank">Download Episode 029: The Pope Of Long Island (with Will Stevemann) (via LibSyn)</a></strong></p>
<p><iframe height="360" scrolling="no" src="//html5-player.libsyn.com/embed/episode/id/3057855/height/360/width/500/theme/standard/direction/no/autoplay/no/autonext/no/thumbnail/yes/preload/no/no_addthis/no/" width="500"></iframe></p>
<p>Typically, this podcast focuses either on Tucson&#8217;s local music/arts community or burritos. Billy Joel does not fall into either category. In fact, I don&#8217;t really have a strong opinion about Billy Joel&#8217;s music either way.</p>
<p><img alt="image" src="http://media.tumblr.com/5157339c0614406bb4dd317b4edae619/tumblr_inline_nbnjsj3nDm1szsob5.png"/></p>
<p><strong>Will Stegemann</strong> does, however.</p>
<p>Will is one of the funniest guys on twitter (<a href="https://twitter.com/BeTheBoy">@BeTheBoy</a>) and we share a major common interest (super-fandom of The Best Show on WFMU). I&#8217;ve always wanted to talk to Will about his up-and-down-and-then-up-again relationship with Billy Joel ever since I became aware of his blog (<a href="http://ayearofbillyjoel.com/">A Year Of Billy Joel</a>). In 2012, Will embarked upon a project to listen to every single piece of Billy Joel music in existence (official albums, pre-solo work, bootlegs, you name it) to fully confront his nemesis head on.</p>
<p>Will HATED Billy Joel when he was younger, which is a sin growing up in Long Island. But a funny thing happened in 2012. This massive Billy Joel project turned into deep self-examination and an exploration of the threads that made up his life &#8212; family, class, his hometown, etc. At some point in the very near future, Will plans to put out a book chronicling his Billy Joel Experience. I can&#8217;t wait.</p>
<p>Will is a wonderful guy to talk to, and this is one of the best interviews we&#8217;ve done on the show. I know you&#8217;ll enjoy this one.</p>
<p>AS AN ADDED BONUS: Take your favorite Billy Joel tune and rewrite the lyrics to be about Mexican food. The winner gets a PRIZE! (no idea what the prize will be yet)</p>

<p><span>Topics: Billy Joel, the absence of moderation, plastic bowling pins, justifiable on-stage meltdowns, his punk record, a new appreciation for the man&#8217;s discography, Amityville, New Year&#8217;s resolutions, improper mastering techniques, seat belts, figuring things out, dogs on twitter, fathers and sons, tonic, gin, feeling completely lost in your early 20s, and the things we do for love.<br/></span></p>

<p>Like millions of other people, I grew up watching The Simpsons over and over and over again. To say it was &#8220;influential&#8221; would be a massive understatement. <p>So.</p>

<p>• Sobriety. I&#8217;m not drinking anymore.</p>
<p>• Feeling less alone in the world.</p>
<p>• <a href="http://www.bedlampublishing.com/LoudZoo_02.pdf">Bedlam Publishing&#8217;s Loud Zoo No. 2</a></p>
<p>• Kelly and I started reading Andy Mascola&#8217;s novels. Great work, Andy!</p>
<p>• New Ty Segall &amp; New Pornographers records. YES!</p>
<p>• Hot rock shows coming up: (Ty Segall Sept. 3, New Pornographers Oct. 14, Free Psych Showcase Aug. 27, HoCo Fest Aug. 30 &#8212; with Prom Body, Katterwaul, and other friends of the podcast).</p>
<p>• Going to see Sun Bones at Che&#8217;s Lounge.</p>
<p>• A Nick original tune: <a href="https://soundcloud.com/nick-prevenas/left-for-good">Left For Good</a>.</p>

<p>Hello and welcome to <strong>Season 2 of the SideStreets Podcast</strong>! We took a week off, but we&#8217;re back and better than ever with this week&#8217;s guests, the extremely sweet and cool <strong>Jordan Prather</strong> and <strong>Belinda Esquer</strong> of the super-cool Tucson band <strong>Head Over Heart</strong>.</p>
<p><img src="http://media.tumblr.com/25149b5246c594f32214c2eb9968f8dc/tumblr_inline_n9walmQ6ZS1szsob5.jpg"/></p>

<p>Many of the bands we&#8217;ve highlighted on this podcast come from a neo-psychedelic/garage-rock persuasion, but Head Over Heart occupies a unique niche in the Tucson music scene. Their sound leans heavily on synths and dance beats, paired with 90s-influenced alt-rock and Britpop. Think of the catchier moments on those XX records, only if they woke up from their nap to put on some Roxy Music or New Order.</p>

<p>We talk about the origins of the band, as well as how they&#8217;ve meshed their individual songwriting styles into this project that doesn&#8217;t quite sound like anything either of them have done before. We also get into their interests in the visual element, their stage set-up, the color purple, and the role Jordan&#8217;s cat (Chicken Man) plays in the group&#8217;s image.</p>
<p><strong>Their debut EP, &#8220;I Believe You, Liar,&#8221; will be officially released on Aug. 9 at Flycatcher (formerly Plush) in Tucson</strong>. See you there!</p>

                                            <h2><a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/92603080512/episode-023-the-breeze-in-our-heads-with-michael-fay">Episode 023: The Breeze In Our Heads (with Michael Fay of Prom Body)</a></h2>
                                            
                                            <div class="body-text">
                                                <p><strong><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850">Subscribe</a><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850"><strong> on iTun</strong>es!</a> (and rate it 5 stars!)</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://traffic.libsyn.com/dryheatpodcast/SideStreetsEpisode023.mp3" target="_blank">Download Episode 023: The Breeze In Our Heads (with Michael Fay of Prom Body) (via LibSyn)</a></strong></p>
<p><iframe height="360" scrolling="no" src="//html5-player.libsyn.com/embed/episode/id/2962261/height/360/width/500/theme/standard/direction/no/autoplay/no/autonext/no/thumbnail/yes/preload/no/no_addthis/no/" width="500"></iframe></p>
<p>Unless you&#8217;re Calexico or Giant Sand, bands from Tucson traditionally don&#8217;t see their singles premiered on Stereogum or their videos shown on NPR.com. <strong><a href="http://prom-body.com/">Prom Body</a></strong> is changing that.</p>
<p><img alt="image" src="http://media.tumblr.com/f103865fc627e0e282ae056b3013ae44/tumblr_inline_n92n6zRLOH1szsob5.jpg"/></p>
<p>Over the past year, <strong>Michael Fay</strong> has seen this bedroom recording project blossom into one of the hottest bands in town &#8212; both from a creative and a commercial standpoint. Prom Body&#8217;s sound is tough to pin down &#8212; a little bit of Ty Segall-style garage, a bit of Wavves-influenced punk, some ambient post-rock thrown into the mix &#8212; but Fay and his crew (<a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/78646741497/sidestreets-podcast-episode-003-all-the-best-stuff-is">including our guest from <strong>Episode 003: Matt Baquet</strong></a>) have established themselves as one of Tucson&#8217;s most promising bands, as well as the ambassador for our town&#8217;s burgeoning music scene as they head out on tour this summer.</p>
<p>Mike and I talk about, well, a little bit of everything in this wide-ranging interview. He discusses his musical background and his songwriting process, as well as his appreciation for how the Tucson music community has come together to help him see this project through. Mike is one of the most insightful young musicians I&#8217;ve ever met, and it was a real pleasure to talk to him.</p>
<p>Prom Body released <a href="http://prombody.bandcamp.com/album/creep-the-strange"><strong>Creep The Strange</strong></a> almost exactly one year ago &#8212; a collection of Fay&#8217;s bedroom recordings when he was living in Kansas City. These songs found a home in Tucson, when he put together a live band. For the past year, with the encouragement of <strong><a href="http://templeofcairo.com">Topaz Records</a></strong> co-founder <strong><a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/82866971437/episode-009-nothing-is-over-with-joel-and-krysta-of">(and guest on Episode 009) Joel Leshefka</a></strong>, Fay has put every ounce of creative energy he can muster into his band&#8217;s second record, <a href="http://www.templeofcairo.com/records/prom-body-naughty-by-natural-lpcass"><strong>Naughty By Natural</strong></a>, which comes out July 29.</p>
<p>The buzz has been deafening. <strong>The single (and official SideStreets Song Of Summer) &#8220;My Paradise&#8221;</strong> was premiered on Vice&#8217;s music blog, Noisey, magazine last month.</p>
<p>• &#8220;<a href="http://noisey.vice.com/blog/prom-body-should-be-your-new-favorite-band-watch-my-paradise-14">Prom Body Should Be Your New Favorite Band</a>" <strong>Noisey</strong>, June 16.</p>
<p>In addition their single &#8220;Pretty Flower&#8221; made its way to Spin Magazine&#8217;s website.</p>
<p>• &#8220;<a href="http://Prom%20Body%20Share%20'Pretty%20Flower,'%20a%20Bouquet%20of%20Garage-Punk%20Goodness">Prom Body Share &#8216;Pretty Flower,&#8217; a Bouquet of Garage-Punk Goodness</a>" <strong>Spin</strong>, May 30.</p>
<p>The momentum kept rolling, as Prom Body&#8217;s phenomenal Nika Kaiser-directed video for the instrumental track &#8220;Guttuggering&#8221; was premiered on NPR. <strong>Bob Boilen called the clip &#8220;jarringly beautiful.&#8221;</strong></p>
<p>• &#8220;<a href="http://www.npr.org/event/music/325901243/prom-body-guttuggering">Prom Body: &#8216;Guttuggering&#8217;</a>" <strong>NPR</strong>, June 27.</p>
<p><iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/qJNClrHwSxk" width="560"></iframe></p>
<p>The biggest break came on July 16, when <a href="http://www.stereogum.com/1692483/prom-body-vibrant-stereogum-premiere/mp3s/">Stereogum premiered the third single, &#8220;Vibrant,&#8221;</a> for its thousands upon thousands of visitors.</p>

                                            <h2><a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/91914604887/episode-022-the-unmagnificent-lives-of-virgin-wolves">Episode 022: The Unmagnificent Lives Of Virgin Wolves (with Andy Mascola)</a></h2>
                                            
                                            <div class="body-text">
                                                <p><strong><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850">Subscribe</a><a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/podcast/the-sidestreets-podcast/id834949850"><strong> on iTun</strong>es!</a> (and rate it 5 stars!)</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://traffic.libsyn.com/dryheatpodcast/SideStreetsEpisode022.mp3" target="_blank">Download Episode 022: The Unmagnificent Lives Of Virgin Wolves (with Andy Mascola) (via LibSyn)</a></strong></p>
<p><iframe height="360" scrolling="no" src="//html5-player.libsyn.com/embed/episode/id/2946415/height/360/width/500/theme/standard/direction/no/autoplay/no/autonext/no/thumbnail/yes/preload/no/no_addthis/no/" width="500"></iframe></p>
<p>You ever have a conversation with someone so genuinely inspired and dedicated that it makes you feel simultaneously lazy (for your own inability to finish projects you start) and invigorated (to actually finish said project)? That&#8217;s how I felt after talking to <strong>Andy Mascola</strong> &#8212; author of the novels <strong>"Unmagnificent Lives"</strong> and <strong>"Who&#8217;s Afraid of the Virgin Wolves?"</strong>.</p>
<p><img alt="image" src="http://media.tumblr.com/8d66d665d8d72332cd507f87284cb901/tumblr_inline_n8rhjf18LD1szsob5.jpg"/></p>
<p>Writing a novel &#8212; let alone two! &#8212; is arduous, demanding work. Andy has managed to follow through on these massive undertakings, while working a day job and being an attentive husband and father. He sleeps very little.</p>
<p>"Dreams are worth pursuing," Andy said. Yes, they are.</p>
<p>We also reference Robert Altman and DMX in the course of the same interview. That&#8217;s fun! Andy is the best &#8212; <strong><a href="https://twitter.com/AndyMascola">follow him on twitter</a></strong> if you don&#8217;t already.</p>
<p>-Buy: <strong><a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1451540116/ref=wl_it_dp_o_pC_nS_ttl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;colid=3W4HOI7OWW1CA&amp;coliid=I1FVJ7GSH09XYE">Unmagnificent Lives</a></strong> (2010)<br/>-Buy: <strong><a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1499285728/ref=wl_it_dp_o_pC_nS_ttl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;colid=3W4HOI7OWW1CA&amp;coliid=I1KQPI1LSCBP3I">Who&#8217;s Afraid of the Virgin Wolves</a></strong> (2014)</p>
<p>Also on today&#8217;s episode:</p>
<p>• Friend of the podcast (and friend of mine in life) Callie Rasmussen (<strong><a href="https://twitter.com/cbRasmussen">@cbRasmussen</a> on twitter</strong>) was responsible for one of my favorite burrito stories back on <a href="http://sidestreetspodcast.tumblr.com/post/86377883562/episode-014-the-years-we-spent-unpressured-with">Episode 014</a>. She checks back in with another wonderful burrito tale &#8212; Boloco (her favorite burrito spot in Vermont) hooked her up with a free burrito, just because they&#8217;re the kindest group of folks imaginable. Boloco gets it. <a href="https://twitter.com/bolocochurchst">Follow them on twitter</a>.</p>
<p>• Our feud scout Cameron (<strong><a href="https://twitter.com/cameron_durkin">@cameron_durkin</a> on twitter</strong>) returns after his hiatus trying to rid the world of Rob Ford. Today, he brings to my attention the fact that <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2014/06/25/beaches-polluted-pollution-water-runoff-sewage-bacteria/11349409/">1 in 10 American beaches are unsafe for swimming</a> (editor&#8217;s note: WHAT?!?!). This is completely unacceptable. Get it together, American beaches &#8212; if not for us, but for Howard Kremer (have a summah!).</p>
<p>• Fresh off the success (??) of last week&#8217;s segment, &#8220;The News Minute: With Stephin Merritt,&#8221; another musician has thrown his hat into the ring to become the podcast&#8217;s official news anchor. Thom Yorke of Radiohead submitted his audition tape, <strong>"The Yorke Report,"</strong> and it&#8217;s&#8230;well&#8230;</p>
<p>• <strong><a href="http://howardkremer.bandcamp.com/">We love Howard Kremer</a></strong> at the SideStreets Podcast.</p>
<p>Music credits:<br/>Bed music: @dashflanagan<br/>The Yorke Report: “Indecipherable” performed (badly) by Nick Prevenas.</p>
<p><strong>Get in touch</strong><span>: Email me at sidestreetspodcast at gmail dot com, or follow me on twitter, either on my </span><a href="https://twitter.com/nprevenas">personal twitter feed</a><span>, or at the </span><a href="https://twitter.com/SideStreetsPod">official SideStreets twitter page</a><span>.</span></p>
<p><span>Topics: Writing, finishing what you start, NaNoWriMo, self publishing, Robert Altman, DMX, unswimmable beaches, people who truly understand burritos, coming up with a workable creative process, chasing a dream, The Yorke Report, summah, and the things we do for love.<br/></span></p>
