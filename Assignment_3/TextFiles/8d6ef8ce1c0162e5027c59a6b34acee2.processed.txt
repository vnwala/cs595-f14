[64]Can I Be Black in Peace?

   Posted on [65]March 14, 2014 by [66]TiffanyAfia

   [67]0

   [68]TiffanyAfia:

     Couldn’t be said any better

   Originally posted on [69]nanahemmaa:

   This morning I read a [70]Daily Mail article– and its ensuing
   comments–about Lupita starring in Shuga, the MTV Kenyan series on HIV
   awareness. The title said it all: Before she was famous: Lingerie-clad
   Oscar winner Lupita Nyong’o juggled two men in sexually-charged Kenyan
   soap opera

   I was beyond angry. I felt so incensed to write a response to the
   stupidity of the article and to the stupidity of the white people who
   chose to participate in the shade-throwing. But I didn’t. I didn’t
   because there was no point. Attempting to discredit Lupita’s talent and
   accomplishments by digging up “dirt” on her “sexually-charged” TV
   series (mind you, not real life), was yet again an attempt to undermine
   women and Black people’s accomplishments. I just could not deal with
   it. How can you hate on HIV awareness? Do you realize it was a
   fictional character she was playing?

   know, have all had the same hair.

   Many people wonder or assume I am mixed race, the occasional few think
   I’ve got a perm.

   I’ve experienced a variety of reactions to my hair ranging from the
   very positive to very negative. I used to focus on the negative ones. I
   hated my hair and thought everything would be ok if only I didn’t have
   it. I also used to wish that I was black or mixed race because then at
   least I wouldn’t have to try and explain my hair to people and wouldn’t
   stand out so much.
