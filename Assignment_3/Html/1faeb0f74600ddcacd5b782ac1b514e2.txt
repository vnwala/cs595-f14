<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://www.bleedingyankeeblue.blogspot.com/2014/09/the-future-of-arod.html'>THE FUTURE OF AROD</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MK_2de1GJZ8/VCm5VDZX6dI/AAAAAAAAhHY/oROGl54VWBc/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BMiami%2Bv%2BFlorida%2BState%2Bw8pzzRNzxLll.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MK_2de1GJZ8/VCm5VDZX6dI/AAAAAAAAhHY/oROGl54VWBc/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BMiami%2Bv%2BFlorida%2BState%2Bw8pzzRNzxLll.jpg" height="640" width="448" /></a></div>
<br />
Sure, the main reason for <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/g/girarjo01.shtml">Joe Girardi's</a> press conference today was to talk about this years Yankee shortcomings.&nbsp; But Alex Rodriguez was a big part of it and something that I'd like to share with all of you.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-bFYgRsa_s-Y/VCm5VdyHHSI/AAAAAAAAhHg/d4-8kFDJ1Ow/s1600/Joe-Girardi-001.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-bFYgRsa_s-Y/VCm5VdyHHSI/AAAAAAAAhHg/d4-8kFDJ1Ow/s1600/Joe-Girardi-001.jpg" height="256" width="400" /></a></div>
At the presser today, <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/mlb/2014/09/29/girardi-a-rod-has-to-show-he-can-still-play/16428299/">Girardi said this about</a> Alex:<br />
<br />
"<i><b>We've got to see where he's physically at, and if he can play the 
field, how many days he can play the field and how many days he needs to
 DH... I don't think really many of us 
know about him until we actually get him into games in spring training... He hasn't played in a year. That's not easy to do, to sit out a year... Do we expect him to be a player on our team? Absolutely. 
Do we expect him to play third base? Yes. But in fairness I think you 
have to see where he's at."</b></i><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-3TjzogwR1ME/VCm5VCzr2nI/AAAAAAAAhHc/qLdfqaI-XEk/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BTampa%2BBay%2BKtOP61dDXhSl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-3TjzogwR1ME/VCm5VCzr2nI/AAAAAAAAhHc/qLdfqaI-XEk/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BTampa%2BBay%2BKtOP61dDXhSl.jpg" height="640" width="417" /></a></div>
<br />
There is no question that a 40 year old with bad hips and who has sat out all season is a liability in the field.&nbsp; Does it mean ARod can come back and impress the Yankees and prove to them that he can still do it? Sure, but the Yankees don't reallly care if he plays the field.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-KBDyJjjuYTM/VCm54n0U8DI/AAAAAAAAhH0/UFDShFZu8zs/s1600/Chase%2BHeadley%2BTexas%2BRangers%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B4SwI3FdxlQgl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-KBDyJjjuYTM/VCm54n0U8DI/AAAAAAAAhH0/UFDShFZu8zs/s1600/Chase%2BHeadley%2BTexas%2BRangers%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B4SwI3FdxlQgl.jpg" height="296" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Sure, it will help them if he plays 3rd, but they know that the bottom line is they will need a third baseman. <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/h/headlch01.shtml">Chase Headley,</a> who I very much enjoyed this year makes a helluva lot of sense.&nbsp; Then on some days, use <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/r/rodrial01.shtml?redir">ARod </a>at third, and DH him the majority of the time.&nbsp; It goes back to what I said before... they don't care about ARod and him in the field.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-swDZHzL81AE/VCm54iRz-hI/AAAAAAAAhHw/-fLCm_5QLFg/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BChicago%2BtGoScA76nH5l.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-swDZHzL81AE/VCm54iRz-hI/AAAAAAAAhHw/-fLCm_5QLFg/s1600/Alex%2BRodriguez%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BChicago%2BtGoScA76nH5l.jpg" height="316" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
They want his bat.&nbsp; If he can hit and hit home runs, at least in 2015, Yankee brass will feel like they will be getting back on this bum investment.&nbsp; That's my opinion of course... but I think I'm right.<br />
<br />
Carry on...<span style="font-size: small;"><b><b>&nbsp;</b></b></span><br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SdnhA1lyadY/VCm-TaYM_7I/AAAAAAAAhII/WK2dfs9HH8Y/s1600/ron-gardenhire-mlb-minnesota-twins-detroit-tigers1-850x560.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SdnhA1lyadY/VCm-TaYM_7I/AAAAAAAAhII/WK2dfs9HH8Y/s1600/ron-gardenhire-mlb-minnesota-twins-detroit-tigers1-850x560.jpg" height="262" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Sad news if you're in Minnesota... or maybe not.&nbsp; Ron Gardenhire, the manager of the Minnesota Twins has been fired.&nbsp; He's been the Twins manager since 2002.&nbsp; He had 8 seasons of winning records, but since 2010, the team has struggled.&nbsp; I guess Gardenhire's the reason. Who knows.<br />
<br />
According to <a href="http://hardballtalk.nbcsports.com/2014/09/29/twins-fire-manager-ron-gardenhire/">Hardball Talk</a>: <i><b>"<a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/managers/gardero01.shtml">Gardenhire</a> replaced <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/managers/kellyto01.shtml">Tom Kelly</a> in 2002 and had immediate success, winning
 six division titles in nine seasons. However, those teams went 6-21 in 
the playoffs. And now the Twins have lost 90 or more games in four 
consecutive seasons and only the Astros have fewer total wins since 
2011."</b></i><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-g36IJ7n7xN8/VCm-TamJoMI/AAAAAAAAhIE/xUXqbMR-jB8/s1600/ron%2Bgard.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-g36IJ7n7xN8/VCm-TamJoMI/AAAAAAAAhIE/xUXqbMR-jB8/s1600/ron%2Bgard.jpg" height="297" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Hey, frustration has set in with Twins management.&nbsp; They are clearly going in a new direction. Ten bucks says they call Tom Kelly just to "check in".<br />
<br />
<span style="font-size: small;">Good Luck Ron.</span><br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://www.bleedingyankeeblue.blogspot.com/2014/09/valuing-jeter-as-person-not-just-yankee.html'>VALUING JETER AS A PERSON... NOT JUST A YANKEE</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
&nbsp;<a class="twitter-hashtag pretty-link js-nav" data-query-source="hashtag_click" dir="ltr" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/ThankYouCaptain?src=hash"><s>#</s><b>ThankYouCaptain</b></a> via <a class="twitter-hashtag pretty-link js-nav" data-query-source="hashtag_click" dir="ltr" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/BYB?src=hash"><s>#</s><b>BYB</b></a><br />
<br />
It's a strange feeling for a writer to be left speechless. We spend so much of our time being able to so eloquently put into words our innermost thoughts and feelings about things, moments... people. Feelings that could&nbsp; keep most completely speechless. Sometimes, believe it or not, I feel that way about Derek Jeter... after all, I'm a fan too. <br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-JC7sFIbDpGc/VCjC5rErjmI/AAAAAAAAhHI/N07cxotOlUc/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BKansas%2BCity%2BRoyals%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BBQy-0hUTCMzl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-JC7sFIbDpGc/VCjC5rErjmI/AAAAAAAAhHI/N07cxotOlUc/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BKansas%2BCity%2BRoyals%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BBQy-0hUTCMzl.jpg" height="400" width="303" /></a></div>
<br />
<div>
I've thought back recently on the career of <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/j/jeterde01.shtml?redir">Derek Jeter</a>. It is littered with so many wonderful moments, it seems nearly impossible to pick one that shines above all others. I think it is the collection of all these moments that make Jeter the icon we see him as. Two moments have been coming back to me more and more. Moments that are so minuscule, they may only be important to me.&nbsp;</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Early in his career, and in a season when I was still too young to care too much about what was happening, Jeter struck out. It wasn't gracefully, either. It was obvious that he did something wrong by his reaction, and by <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/t/torrejo01.shtml">Joe Torre'</a>s reaction. Torre, who usually was quite stone faced, was obviously annoyed by his young shortstop.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-k5WVi1XKTww/VCjCp9Ou4xI/AAAAAAAAhHA/DoGGSfTJsBk/s1600/alg-jeter-torre-jpg.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-k5WVi1XKTww/VCjCp9Ou4xI/AAAAAAAAhHA/DoGGSfTJsBk/s1600/alg-jeter-torre-jpg.jpg" height="305" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
When Jeter made it to the dugout, where other players would pitch fits by themselves over their mistakes, Jeter sat next to Torre, and welcomed the reprimand. He took in what Torre told him, and made the adjustments he needed to make. He didn't expect to be forgiven, or for his mistake to be overlooked. He took the criticism, and worked to change.<br />
</div>
<div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AYf3pLIdhyM/VCjCjy-L37I/AAAAAAAAhG4/lqESTthHdhk/s1600/02.derek-jeter.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AYf3pLIdhyM/VCjCjy-L37I/AAAAAAAAhG4/lqESTthHdhk/s1600/02.derek-jeter.jpg" height="400" width="332" /></a></div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Everything since that moment has been a result of hard work. &nbsp;Jeter has literally put blood, sweat and tears into his career. Never satisfied with settling. The wins and losses were always part of the bigger picture. His ultimate goal was always the World Series.&nbsp;</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
The second moment was more recently. A couple of years ago, I had the opportunity to sit behind the Yankees dugout. I was in awe, to say the least. But the thing that stayed with me the most was Jeter's presence on the field. When it came time for the team to get out there, he didn't yell "<b>GO</b>."<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-As-iLaas9xk/VCjCMvTrtyI/AAAAAAAAhGw/-NYY7FQwcsY/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBoston%2BRed%2BSox%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BBX15wKuaeIjl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-As-iLaas9xk/VCjCMvTrtyI/AAAAAAAAhGw/-NYY7FQwcsY/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBoston%2BRed%2BSox%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BBX15wKuaeIjl.jpg" height="286" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
He became the complete embodiment of a leader and said "<i><b>Let's Go</b></i>," being the first one out onto the field every time!&nbsp;</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
We live in a society where many have such a sense of entitlement, it's a little bit disheartening. It seems like the people who value hard work, and leadership are few and far in-between. Jeter has always been an incredible example of both those things. It's really simple for a guy like Jeter to coast. He's gained the popularity, and respect. Why not allow others to do for him?<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-dD-EbArBFtA/VCjB_KMbKrI/AAAAAAAAhGo/Sy_LraQT5pk/s1600/dj_3000.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-dD-EbArBFtA/VCjB_KMbKrI/AAAAAAAAhGo/Sy_LraQT5pk/s1600/dj_3000.jpg" height="297" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Because that isn't who Jeter is! He's a running full on down the first baseline to beat the out kind of guy. He's an emerging from the tunnel first to set an example kind of guy. He's a dive into the stands, flip play home plate making, 3,000th hit record setting kind of guy.&nbsp;</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Maybe my admiration for handwork, dedication and leadership is why I adore Jeter, or maybe it's because of Jeter that I value those things. I'm not entirely certain, and that is completely acceptable.<br />
<br />
But I do know that Jeter is all the things I value. Not in the Yankee, but in the person.<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcGNPXccnI0/UsJZS5m9CnI/AAAAAAAAAcg/a2sXtRZClVY/s1600/1379273333-erica%2Bmorales1.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcGNPXccnI0/UsJZS5m9CnI/AAAAAAAAAcg/a2sXtRZClVY/s1600/1379273333-erica%2Bmorales1.jpg" /></a></div>

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dMsC-Csa7Bc/VCizhg7YnXI/AAAAAAAAhFE/qsrFQEz8Zd0/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2B6qV4rB0XBQEl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dMsC-Csa7Bc/VCizhg7YnXI/AAAAAAAAhFE/qsrFQEz8Zd0/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2B6qV4rB0XBQEl.jpg" height="255" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
There is never an easy way to say goodbye, but it happened today in Fenway Park.&nbsp; And, I'm happy to say, that as a Yankee fan, there was nothing that made me more proud as a Yankee fan, than to share it with Red Sox Nation.&nbsp; I know, it sounds corny and ridiculous and yet, it was the perfect place for the send off of Derek Jeter.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-l1Dn26Vpz_U/VCiyt09RBWI/AAAAAAAAhEw/nE8fhUe9lIU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2Bl5lNCamqp6kl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-l1Dn26Vpz_U/VCiyt09RBWI/AAAAAAAAhEw/nE8fhUe9lIU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2Bl5lNCamqp6kl.jpg" height="266" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
I don't know. Maybe it was the 9-5 win.&nbsp; Maybe it was just the fact that the Red Sox fans respected<a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/j/jeterde01.shtml?redir"> Derek Jeter</a> the past 3 days.&nbsp; Who knows what it was. The reality is, while the rivalry is still there... that didn't matter this weekend.&nbsp; Instead, the happiness surrounding Jeter resonated with everyone.&nbsp; It was absolutely amazing. As a Yankee fan, you can't thank the Red Sox fans enough.&nbsp; You are fans like us and it was never more clearer than today.<br />
<br />
I don't want to do a full blown recap today, other than to let you know that the Yankees won.&nbsp; Here's the reality; The win doesn't matter. The day as a whole DOES.<br />
<br />
Boston's best gave Jeter his final send-off and plenty in the crowd that were Yankee fans too.&nbsp; It was great to see.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P-Ul_Bl4jt8/VCiR5Xnr3PI/AAAAAAAAhEc/anfHP8r2OuE/s1600/mothers%2Blove.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P-Ul_Bl4jt8/VCiR5Xnr3PI/AAAAAAAAhEc/anfHP8r2OuE/s1600/mothers%2Blove.png" height="233" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Seeing Mrs. Jeter for a moment at Fenway brought it full circle. Someone tweeted this photo today and wrote "<i><b>A mother's love is priceless."</b></i> Nailed it.&nbsp; It was right then that I lost it.&nbsp; We have expressed at BYB just how wonderful family is and can be, and when it's a family like Derek's, with the teaching of determination, hard work, pride, professionalism, well it's no wonder our guy Derek succeeded as much as he has his entire career.<br />
<br />
BYB writer Suzie Pinstripes was there today, and tweeted this:<br />
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
That's a wrap! <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/FarewellCaptain?src=hash">#FarewellCaptain</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/dRunner26_2">@dRunner26_2</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/BleednYankeeBlu">@BleednYankeeBlu</a> <a href="http://t.co/ia3IZyLzXH">pic.twitter.com/ia3IZyLzXH</a><br />
&#8212; Suzanne  (@suzieprof) <a href="https://twitter.com/suzieprof/status/516293850684727296">September 28, 2014</a></blockquote>
<script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script>

She'll have an in depth piece about her experience at Fenway, as well as the reaction to the Captain. I can't wait to see it.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ykb6wntDWAc/VCiyttkttcI/AAAAAAAAhEs/Ah37r8yNK0E/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BhFN4OEQwgUol.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ykb6wntDWAc/VCiyttkttcI/AAAAAAAAhEs/Ah37r8yNK0E/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BhFN4OEQwgUol.jpg" height="261" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
As <a href="http://mlb.mlb.com/mlb/gameday/index.jsp?gid=2014_09_28_nyamlb_bosmlb_1&amp;mode=box&amp;partnerId=LR_box#gid=2014_09_28_nyamlb_bosmlb_1&amp;mode=recap&amp;c_id=nyy">Bryan Hoch</a> had it today: <i><b>"Jeter's season-long goodbye ended in fitting fashion on Sunday 
afternoon, running hard down the first-base line to notch the 3,465th 
hit of his career. He signaled for a pinch-runner and waved his batting 
helmet as he left the field, exiting a game that the Yankees went on to 
win, 9-5.</b></i><br />
<br />
<i><b>'I felt like the time was right,' Jeter said. 'My emotions were so 
all over the place on Thursday in New York. When I got here, I was 
ready. I was ready for my career to be over with. I'm happy I had an 
opportunity to come and play here in a couple games, but I'm ready for 
this to be the end.'"</b></i><br />
<br />
That quote is very significant.&nbsp; We've seen big time players hang it up only to come back soon after and fade out worse than they did the first time. Brett Favre comes to mind.&nbsp; Others just need another year or 2 just to be sure. Andy Pettitte comes to mind. Jeter, always strategic with how he presents himself knew it was time. For the critics that ask why he announced at the beginning of the season. Well, I loved the idea. For him, it was a way to organize his priorities for the season.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fT3ukJbRUW8/VCi0ghinDbI/AAAAAAAAhFM/Tw9cU1oue4w/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BZWJTC9KuuSql.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fT3ukJbRUW8/VCi0ghinDbI/AAAAAAAAhFM/Tw9cU1oue4w/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BZWJTC9KuuSql.jpg" height="266" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Could you imagine if we didn't know Jeter was going to retire until today? Yankeeland would be devastated.&nbsp; The more I think about the way Jeter handled it, announcing the beginning of the season... he did it right.<br />
<br />
And there was a moment today, after <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/m/mccanbr01.shtml">Brian McCann</a> came in to pinch run for Derek, Derek was crossing the infield and walked up to the mound.&nbsp; <br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-jlnORKUW7Vk/VCiytlj6yKI/AAAAAAAAhE0/iCkC77wDog0/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BqxbAtBsrshdl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-jlnORKUW7Vk/VCiytlj6yKI/AAAAAAAAhE0/iCkC77wDog0/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2BqxbAtBsrshdl.jpg" height="278" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
He stopped and shook <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/b/buchhcl01.shtml">Clay Buchholz </a>hand.&nbsp; Again, according to Hoch, Derek said to Clay,<i><b> 'I know this is kind of odd but I just wanted to say I've enjoyed competing against you over the years and good luck.' </b></i>He's a class act.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3LHW6Ch2J1A/VCi0rsq-EUI/AAAAAAAAhFU/iAGS8rAaKqU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BtN_vXo6qkNFl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3LHW6Ch2J1A/VCi0rsq-EUI/AAAAAAAAhFU/iAGS8rAaKqU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BtN_vXo6qkNFl.jpg" height="270" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
BYB has been reflecting on Jeter for the past week.&nbsp; Many of our writers have put together some pretty incredible pieces.&nbsp; We needed to do this, because despite the haters, it's evident, Derek Jeter is king.&nbsp; Derek Jeter is the greatest. It was never for evident than his final night in the Bronx... and his final day ever, in Fenway Park in Boston today.&nbsp; To see baseball celebrate this man as much as we have...well,&nbsp; it's emotional.&nbsp; I was touched by a tweet from my friend Danny today:<br />
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
<a href="https://twitter.com/BleednYankeeBlu">@BleednYankeeBlu</a> Somehow I cannot comprehend that I am watching Derek Jeter's last game in the major leagues.... <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/Yankees?src=hash">#Yankees</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/Re2pect?src=hash">#Re2pect</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/HOF2020?src=hash">#HOF2020</a><br />
&#8212; Danny O Brion (@DannyOBrion) <a href="https://twitter.com/DannyOBrion/status/516278230203260928">September 28, 2014</a></blockquote>
<script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script>

That's about explains it.&nbsp; It's gonna be hard to not see Jeter out there at shortstop next year for the New York Yankees.&nbsp; He's been a fixture for 20 years.&nbsp; He's our leader. He's our guy; Homegrown, pinstripe pride and always clutch from the start to the end of this career.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-6tn2Yob7F5M/VCi1GoEWcRI/AAAAAAAAhFc/ru3iuonjen8/s1600/jeter-four.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-6tn2Yob7F5M/VCi1GoEWcRI/AAAAAAAAhFc/ru3iuonjen8/s1600/jeter-four.jpeg" height="400" width="306" /></a></div>
<br />
But through all of this, and we've talked about this alot here, it's his family structure that brought him to greatness.&nbsp; Sure, applaud Jeter as a man and all that he did with the New York Yankees.&nbsp; After all, he's a terrific ballplayer and an amazing role model.&nbsp; But here's a fact... leaders are created by incredible parents.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cpfGzl-9VrM/VCi2NYScj4I/AAAAAAAAhFo/JDNvAa7jfiw/s1600/1411581240000-USP-MLB-Kansas-City-Royals-at-New-York-Yankees.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cpfGzl-9VrM/VCi2NYScj4I/AAAAAAAAhFo/JDNvAa7jfiw/s1600/1411581240000-USP-MLB-Kansas-City-Royals-at-New-York-Yankees.jpg" height="300" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Dot and the Doc are extraordinary people, always there to guide Derek.&nbsp; They were clearly in his head throughout his life, allowing him to lead, helping him to make smart choices and most of all, they offered values that allowed the man to blossom into a determined young man, with goals, with ambition and with heart in whatever he wanted to accomplish in life.&nbsp; Sure, tip your cap to the Cap... but applaud the Jeter parents on this one.&nbsp; Without them... we may not see this great man before us. You know what I mean?<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-suujJhOtrd0/VCi27oMqSlI/AAAAAAAAhFw/WiG_e-Oddj8/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2B5qL3KkZMyHgl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-suujJhOtrd0/VCi27oMqSlI/AAAAAAAAhFw/WiG_e-Oddj8/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2Bv%2BBoston%2BRed%2B5qL3KkZMyHgl.jpg" height="281" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Finally, let me say this.&nbsp; Today was hard.&nbsp; Many cried and millions were sad seeing this man hang in up.&nbsp; But it's not goodbye.&nbsp; The greatest part about Derek Jeter, <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/p/posadjo01.shtml">Jorge Posada</a>, <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/p/pettian01.shtml">Andy Pettitte</a>, <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/r/riverma01.shtml">Mariano Rivera </a>and <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/w/willibe02.shtml">Bernie Williams</a> is they're Yankees for life.&nbsp; They'll always be there. Old Timers Day. Any Yankee celebrations we have at the Stadium. Hell, maybe one day Derek Jeter will own the Yankees.&nbsp; The point is, while it's <b><i>"So Long",</i></b> it's not <i><b>"Goodbye".</b></i>&nbsp; Luckily for us... Jeter and the boys will always be there.<br />
<br />
And remember something ladies and gentlemen.&nbsp; You're sad... and you can't help it... but don't be sad because it's over. Be glad that you witnessed greatness.&nbsp; It's a privilege, a thrill and I'm emotional, but overjoyed to have seen Jeter play all these years.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lghy9vyBpwc/VCi33J9pvOI/AAAAAAAAhF8/uWk3tNbpuMk/s1600/2.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lghy9vyBpwc/VCi33J9pvOI/AAAAAAAAhF8/uWk3tNbpuMk/s1600/2.png" height="372" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Thank you Cap. You did it right... and we'll always remember the good times.&nbsp; Shortstop in Yankee Stadium will never be the same #2, but you did us proud, and we'll always love you Cap.<br />
<br />
5 Championships. 3465 hits.&nbsp; Atta boy kid.<br />
<br />

<a href='http://www.bleedingyankeeblue.blogspot.com/2014/09/o-captain-my-captain_28.html'>O CAPTAIN, MY CAPTAIN!</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<a class="twitter-hashtag pretty-link js-nav" data-query-source="hashtag_click" dir="ltr" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/ThankYouCaptain?src=hash" style="background-color: #121226; color: #f0d78c; font-family: Georgia, Utopia, 'Palatino Linotype', Palatino, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2000007629395px; text-decoration: none;"><s>#</s><b>ThankYouCaptain</b></a><span style="background-color: #121226; color: white; font-family: Georgia, Utopia, 'Palatino Linotype', Palatino, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2000007629395px;">&nbsp;via&nbsp;</span><a class="twitter-hashtag pretty-link js-nav" data-query-source="hashtag_click" dir="ltr" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/BYB?src=hash" style="background-color: #121226; color: #f0d78c; font-family: Georgia, Utopia, 'Palatino Linotype', Palatino, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2000007629395px; text-decoration: none;"><s>#</s><b>BYB</b></a><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-a5iRpY9ri9Q/VCbr08lG-hI/AAAAAAAAhBc/5JhqwRhlMU4/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BUg-g72kyxs1l.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-a5iRpY9ri9Q/VCbr08lG-hI/AAAAAAAAhBc/5JhqwRhlMU4/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2BUg-g72kyxs1l.jpg" height="263" width="400" /></a></div>
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-RwauXkgfsI8/VCbr02pfTUI/AAAAAAAAhCE/mfZv30nc6XU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B7WPl49huNvOl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"></a> <br />
<br />
Sunday we say goodbye to El Capitan. And I, for one, am devastated; but probably for different reasons than most of you. Look, Derek is an amazing ballplayer and based on the current state of professional athletes, <a href="http://bleedingyankeeblue.blogspot.com/2014/09/role-models-jeter-respect-pass-it-on.html">a role model for kids everywhere</a>. I&#8217;m not denying what he&#8217;s meant to the game, the Yankees, and fans in general but for me it&#8217;s about something deeper.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0fptwTRNmEQ/VCbyvCpJlhI/AAAAAAAAhCU/GbwUnOKKfkk/s1600/don%2Bmattingly1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0fptwTRNmEQ/VCbyvCpJlhI/AAAAAAAAhCU/GbwUnOKKfkk/s1600/don%2Bmattingly1.jpg" height="332" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
As stated in many of my other stories, I grew up idolizing <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/m/mattido01.shtml">Don Mattingly.</a> And throughout most of his career (and my childhood) the Yankees were terrible. He was the only bright spot in a rather bleak era of Yankees lore. So, I get it.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nXL6sIKSthc/VCbyvOzrE2I/AAAAAAAAhCY/pfBDQyy_4rM/s1600/don%2Bmattingly%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nXL6sIKSthc/VCbyvOzrE2I/AAAAAAAAhCY/pfBDQyy_4rM/s1600/don%2Bmattingly%2B2.jpg" height="300" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
I get the <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/m/mattido01.shtml">Derek Jeter</a>-mania. For an entire generation of fans Derek Jeter is to them what Don Mattingly was to me. How many kids went to Little League and asked for #2? How many girls do you see wearing a Jeter shirt? Derek Jeter is all they know. <b>Since 1996, Derek Jeter has been the (pretty) face of baseball and the Yankees.</b><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-rNIMejCpeaQ/VCbr17h_DJI/AAAAAAAAhB0/91WveYHafVI/s1600/joreg%2Bposada.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-rNIMejCpeaQ/VCbr17h_DJI/AAAAAAAAhB0/91WveYHafVI/s1600/joreg%2Bposada.jpg" height="400" width="292" /></a></div>
<br />
For me, once Mattingly retired I gravitated towards <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/o/o%27neipa01.shtml">Paul O&#8217;Neill</a>, <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/p/posadjo01.shtml">Jorge Posada</a>, <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/r/riverma01.shtml">Mariano Rivera</a>. Why? Maybe because those guys played hard. Maybe because those guys, at the time, weren&#8217;t the popular choice. Maybe because Jeter has always been a &#8220;pretty boy&#8221;; an easy choice for fans everywhere. But maybe for me, it was just to be different. Jeter was the obvious successor to the Yankees chain of gloriousness and being an awkward teenager at the time drove me to do the opposite of what everyone else was doing.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-rJhabWaYzOg/VCbr1uX-2GI/AAAAAAAAhBs/hb8ZYOuFhC8/s1600/frank-thomas.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-rJhabWaYzOg/VCbr1uX-2GI/AAAAAAAAhBs/hb8ZYOuFhC8/s1600/frank-thomas.jpg" height="320" width="320" /></a></div>
<br />
I guess that was my anti-establishment phase. Remember, I always took<a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/t/thomafr04.shtml"> Frank Thomas</a> in the Thomas/<a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/g/griffke02.shtml">Griffey </a>argument.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-K9k9wca6smc/VCbr04oILRI/AAAAAAAAhBg/1EnIdDi938E/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBoston%2BRed%2BSox%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B2lVhnuj3Uhhl.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-K9k9wca6smc/VCbr04oILRI/AAAAAAAAhBg/1EnIdDi938E/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBoston%2BRed%2BSox%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B2lVhnuj3Uhhl.jpg" height="640" width="449" /></a></div>
<br />
Flash Forward to 2014, I am a 35-year-old father of 2. All of my heroes from childhood are long-since retired and right now, Derek Jeter is the lone connection to my younger days and now that he&#8217;s retiring; once he&#8217;s gone so will that connection to those times. Growing old is a part of life, a part I&#8217;m having a hard time accepting at the moment. Jeter was never a personal hero but his retirement will leave me with a sense of loss. A sense that time is passing me, all of us, by. Nothing lasts forever.<br />
<br />
Wow, it just got dark there for a bit. Sorry about that.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-RwauXkgfsI8/VCbr02pfTUI/AAAAAAAAhCE/mfZv30nc6XU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B7WPl49huNvOl.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-RwauXkgfsI8/VCbr02pfTUI/AAAAAAAAhCE/mfZv30nc6XU/s1600/Derek%2BJeter%2BBaltimore%2BOrioles%2Bv%2BNew%2BYork%2BYankees%2B7WPl49huNvOl.jpg" height="400" width="285" /></a></div>
<br />
To Derek, thank you. Enjoy your retirement, it&#8217;s well deserved. You&#8217;ve always done what&#8217;s right and played the game hard. You&#8217;ve&nbsp;&nbsp;been a constant for 20 years. We don&#8217;t see that much with professional athletes these days. While you&#8217;re leaving a select few with gift baskets, I will be left with a sense of loss and despair. Baseball is a child&#8217;s game and every child grows up. Now it&#8217;s time for me to grow up.<br />
<br />
