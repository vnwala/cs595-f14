<a href='http://www.projectrori.com/2014/06/desucon-9-norway.html'>Desucon 9 NORWAY </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-601395680098340400' itemprop='articleBody'>
So, hi! You wonder what this is? This is my yearly post about the Norwegian convention "Desucon".<br />
<br />
I'm... so blown away by this year, that I honestly don't know what to write! We had thousands of visitors and this year, Desucon was over 3 days. Well more like 2 and a half day, since it started at 3 p.m. on Friday and ended at 8 p.m. on Sunday!<br />
<br />
This year, I saw so many great cosplays and I saw two that I really liked, which was Blake and Yang from the American series, RWBY. I was looking for those two the whole weekend, that I almost forgot to take pictures, so that's why I don't have many pictures for you guys this year. Right, I was also looking for someone cosplaying as Takamina from the AKB0048 anime. I'm so sad I didn't find her, because I wanted to meet a fellow AKB-fan! So few of us her in Norway ;..;<br />
<br />
Anyhow, once again I was in the crew, but as last year, I wasn't security. I was facility, which is logistics and services together. We basically take care of the food for the crew and pick up thrash from the visitors. It's an ok job.<br />
<br />
Kay... Back to cosplays. I also saw quite a few crossplays this year, among them was a guy dressing up as Madoka from Puella Magi Madoka Magika. I'm sure I saw a few others, but I honestly don't remember them much xD There was also a bunch of League of Legends-cosplays this year, I got to take some pictures of them. I also heard there was a Tryndamere at the con this year, but didn't see him :/<br />
<br />
There was so many great stands this year too and all those artstands! I wanted to buy everything, but I didn't have enough money :( Ended up with 5 new figurines, a new poster, lightsaber umbrella (green) and two different pillows (one squared and one long). I actually bought this cute hat, with cat-ears on and some... long things on the sides, where I could put my hands. I ended up going around and creep behind other crew-members and make weird animal-sounds. Most of them laughed!<br />
<br />
Anyways, I'm still tired from Desucon so I'm gonna end my post here. Enjoy my pictures! :D<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://www.projectrori.com/2014/05/im-so-lazy.html'>I'm so lazy!</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7337609676008571710' itemprop='articleBody'>
Howdy! I'm really sorry that I haven't posted anything since mid October. I've really wanted to, but there have been a lot stuff going that have disturbed me from posting stuff, but I promise y'all that I will get more active in the months to come.<br />
<hr />
First of all: I have yet to complete DokiDoki PreCure, so don't expect a review or blabbering for that one anytime soon. I'm still on episode 29 or something, so yeah. I've grown pretty much tired of the Pretty Cure-series and that is also the reason why I haven't even bothered to start watching the new one and I probably won't even start on it. Once I've completed DokiDoki and Yes, Pretty Cure 5! GoGo!, I won't watch anymore, since 10 seasons is more than enough for me. <br />
So, I want to say a few words about the current season of anime, the spring season 2014. It's a few good series now, among them that is worth mentioning is: <b>Inugami-san to Nekoyama-san</b>, <b>Hitsugi no Chaika</b>, <b>Akuma no Riddle</b>, <b>Love Live! School Idol Project 2nd Season </b>, <b>Kanojo ga Flag wo Oraretara</b> and <b>Date a Live II</b>. Well, those are what I am currently watching, including some other longer series, like <b>Naruto Shippuuden</b>, <b>Aikatsu</b> and the show that started during the previous season, <b>Nisekoi</b>. <br />
If you are still unsure what you should watch this season, I really recommend you to start watching Hitsugi no Chaika, Akuma no Riddle and Inugami-san to Nekoyama-san. The two latter ones contains a good portion of yuri! So if you like that, watch them. I can mention that Inugami-san to Nekoyama-san is only 5 minutes per episodes! <br />
If you want harem, you should watch Kanojo ga Flag wo Oraretara and the second season for Date a Live (you should watch the first season first). There is a strong possibility that Hitsugi no Chaika will turn into a harem series of some sort. If you are into idol-series, you should watch Love Live and Aikatsu. Both of them are on the second season now, though myanimelist have listed Aikatsu as one season with now over 70 episodes! But it's great! You should watch Aikatsu anyways. Anyone will like it. Some final words: If you are into "older" anime series, you should check Dragon Half from the 80's and Malice@Doll from the early 2000's. I watched Malice@Doll yesterday and I must say, it was the most weirdest and creepiest anime I've ever seen! Malice@Doll compared to series like Higurashi, Umineko and Elfen Lied, Malice@Doll doesn't have any gore like Higurashi and Umineko have. What it does have though, is some great 3DCG and good characters! Hey, thanks for reading and see ya laters! <br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1357664077978968155' itemprop='articleBody'>
Yeah, it's that time of the year already, where I write about the norwegian convention Desucon. The con was &nbsp;for the first time held in Lillestrøm, at&nbsp;Norway Trade Fairs. Sadly, it's still two days. One positive side of having it at&nbsp;Norway Trade Fairs, is that Desucon now has a LOT more room.&nbsp;Norway Trade Fairs can contain about 9 1/2 Oslo Congress Centre, where the con have been held earlier.<br />
<br />
On another note, for the first time since Desucon 3 in 2008, I've been something else than security. This time around I was in the&nbsp;logistics-crew. I really enjoyed that. It was a nice change and I'm propably going to stick with the crew for another few years.<br />
<br />
There was A LOT of FANTASTIC cosplays at this year Desucon, one example is&nbsp;<a href="http://tinemarieriis.blogg.no/1372965399_desucon_8_2013.html">Tine Marie</a>&nbsp;(norwegian text), which dressed up as Spellthied Lux and Miss Fortune from League of Legends.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ElffiCosplay">Elffi Cosplay</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/calssara.cosplay">Calssara Cosplay</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/KamuiCos">Kamui</a>&nbsp;paid us a visit! Elffi and Kamui had their own workshops during the con and Elffi had a Q&amp;A at the same time. Calssara and Elffi also did "Cosplay around the World" were they spoke of their experience of attending other conventions and stuff. I couldn't see that one, as I was pretty busy most of the time doing logistic-stuff.<br />
<br />
Anyways, there goes another fantastic Desucon and I really enjoyed myself! There was so much I wanted to buy! I saw a Makise Kurisu (Steins;Gate) figurine I wanted, but by the time I could buy it, it was sold out! I ended up buying 6 new figurines, a new Hatsune Miku-poster, Hatsune Miku-bag and a drinking bottle-thing with chibi Miku on! I'll post a picture of my new figurines, they will appear on the bottom of the blog post!<br />
